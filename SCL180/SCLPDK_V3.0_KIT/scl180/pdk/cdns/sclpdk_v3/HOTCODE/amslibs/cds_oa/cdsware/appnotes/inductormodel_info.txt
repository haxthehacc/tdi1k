Skin Effect Model Information

The scalable broad-band physically based inductor models are generally 
accurate when computing the inductance and capacitance of the structure. 
The resistive losses though tend to be less accurate.  Two models
are provided for the resistive losses.

The Parallel RL model uses a resistor in parallel with the inductor
to represent the skin effect.  Historical validation, experience,
and legacy has shown that the model is adequately accurate for RFIC 
design; however, it is accurate up to frequecies where the skin depth 
is greater than half the metal thickness as stated in Equation 1.

        2 * skin depth > metal thickness  (eq 1)

The Resistor Ladder is a set of parallel RL series components as its
namesake implies.  The internal inductance of the coil is computed and 
used with the DC resistance to create a ladder that approximates the 
impact of the skin effect.

At low frequencies where Equation 1 is satisfied (e.g.  f < 2.5GHz
for 3um of Al), the Parallel RL model can be accurate to a bit 
pessimistic (up to -20%) and the Resistor Ladder can be accurate to a 
bit optimistic (up to +20%).

At higher frequencies where Equation 1 is not satisfied (e.g. f > 2.5GHz
for 3um of Al), the Parallel RL model is very pessimistic (as much as 
-50% to -100%) and the Resistor Ladder can be either pessimistic or 
optimistic (+/- 20%).
