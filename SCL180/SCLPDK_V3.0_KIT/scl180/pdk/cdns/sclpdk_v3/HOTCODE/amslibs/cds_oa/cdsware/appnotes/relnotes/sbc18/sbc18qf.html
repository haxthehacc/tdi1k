* The SBC18QE family of processes has four layers of aluminum metal including 
  dual, thick metals for M3 and M4. These processes are built on high resistivity 
  substrates with nominal resistivity of either 50 (QE1/QF) or 1000 (QF2) 
  ohms-cm. They support 
   (a) 1.8 and 5V CMOS 
   (b) 6V and 8V SiGe HBTs 
   (c) unsilicided poly resistors 
   (d) a 2 fF/um2 MIM capacitor on M2 and (e) inductors using the thick 
       aluminum top metal layers.
* There are five new unique features in this process family not generally 
  found in other SBC18 processes:
   (1) The addition of the TSV module (QE1 only) which connects M1 
       to the back side of the silicon wafer through a rectangular tungsten-
       filled via
   (2) The two SiGe HBTs designed for operation at higher voltages as 
       compared with those available in other SBC18 processes
   (3) The use of dual thick metal layers on the top two metal layers. 
       M3 is 2.8um thick Aluminum in all cases and M4 is either 2.8 (QE1/QF)
       or 5.2um (QF2) thick Aluminum.
   (4) The use of slotted via3s to connect the thick top metal layers.
   (5) The use of high resistivity starting material
